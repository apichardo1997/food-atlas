Methodology

Food Similarity Index

We measure bilateral food similarity using the Finger-Kreinin index (overlap in distribution method). Food items are grouped into meat (30%), starch (30%), and vegetables (40%). For each country pair, similarity is the weighted sum of minimum shares across all food items.

Climate Similarity Index

We apply the same overlap method to climate. For each country, we compute the share of land area in each of the 30 Koppen-Geiger climate zones. The similarity between two countries is:

Similarityij = Σ min(siz, sjz) x 100

where s_iz is country i's share of land in zone z. Scores range from 0 (completely different) to 100 (identical).

Data Sources

Climate: Koppen-Geiger classification at 0.1 degree resolution (1991-2020) from Beck et al. (2023). Food: FAO Food Balance Sheets (kcal/capita/day). Trade & gravity: CEPII Gravity, BACI HS22. GDP: World Bank. Country polygons: Natural Earth (ne_countries).
